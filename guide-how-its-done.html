how it's done

SEM with an agent, step by step.

The demo script, reproducible at any company. An agent reads your website, researches, writes the ads, structures the campaign and exports a CSV ready to upload. What a team takes days to do, in minutes.

What you need

An assistant with web and search access, your website URL and —optional, for the finishing touch— Google Ads Editor and a Google Ads account. The CSV is imported and stays as a draft: in the demo nothing is published and nothing is spent.

The 5 steps
01
Read the website
The agent understands the business on its own: what it sells and to whom.
prompt
Read [your-site]. In 4 lines: what it sells, to whom,
value proposition and the 3 main services.
02
Research keywords and competitors
What the customer searches for and how the rivals position themselves.
prompt
Find how your competitors position themselves in Google
Search: what intent they target and what messaging they use.
03
Build the campaign
The key moment: structure, keywords, ads and negatives.
prompt
Build a Search campaign in Google Ads for [your-site].
Market and language: [your country] / [your language]. I want:
- 6-8 single-theme ad groups, each with its intent.
- Keywords in phrase and exact match (no broad).
- Per group, 1 responsive ad (RSA) with 12-15 headlines
  (max 30 characters) and 4 descriptions (max 90), with the
  keyword in at least 3 headlines, a benefit and a call to
  action.
- 15-20 negative keywords.
- 4 sitelinks, 4 callouts and 1 structured snippet.
- Bidding strategy and daily budget to start.
Give it back as a table and prepare a CSV compatible with
Google Ads Editor.
04
Export the CSV
Ready to import into Google Ads Editor.
prompt
Generate the Google Ads Editor CSV (Campaign, Ad Group,
Keyword, Match Type, etc.) and give it to me to download.
05
Optimize the landing page
The agent tells you how to pay less per click.
prompt
Audit [your-site] as a landing page for these keywords:
the 5 changes that most raise Quality Score and lower CPC.
The agent builds, the human switches it on

In the demo nothing is published and no money is spent: the CSV is imported and stays as a draft. The agent builds and optimizes; the person approves and decides what to switch on.

From 5 prompts to 1 command

The real shortcut is to encapsulate this script as a reusable skill: that turns five prompts into a single command —"build SEM for this website"— that anyone on the team can invoke.

Honest fine print
  • Skills = the assistant + its skills mechanism. There's no out-of-the-box "SEM" button: you encapsulate the playbook yourself.
  • Validate the CSV headers against your version of Google Ads Editor (they change between versions). Import into a test account first.
  • Real volumes and CPC come from Keyword Planner with your account. The agent estimates; you refine the bids.
  • It's a build accelerator, not investment advice. The judgment of how much and when to spend is human.
