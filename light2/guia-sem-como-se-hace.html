how it's done

SEM with an agent, step by step.

The demo script, repeatable at any company. An agent reads your site, researches, writes the ads, structures the campaign, uploads it to Google Ads — paused — and hands you the keyword study. A week's work, in minutes.

What you need

An assistant with web and search access, your website URL, and a Google Ads account with API access (developer token). The campaign is genuinely uploaded through the API, but it lands PAUSED: no ad is published and not a cent is spent until a person switches it on.

The 6 steps
01
Read the website
The agent figures out the business on its own: what it sells and to whom.
prompt
Read [your-site]. In 4 lines: what it sells, to whom,
value proposition and the 3 main services.
02
Research keywords and competitors
What the customer searches for and how rivals position themselves.
prompt
Look up how the competitors of [your-site] rank in
Google Search: what intent they target and what messaging
they use.
03
Write the ads
Headlines and descriptions that respect Google's limits.
prompt
For each group, write 1 responsive search ad (RSA): 12-15
headlines (max 30 characters) and 4 descriptions (max 90),
with the group's keyword in at least 3 headlines, plus
benefit and call to action.
04
Structure the campaign and export the CSV
Groups, match types, negatives and extensions → CSV ready to upload.
prompt
Structure the Search campaign for [your-site] in
[your country] / [your language]. I want:
- 6-8 single-theme ad groups, each with its intent.
- Keywords in phrase and exact match (no broad).
- 15-20 negative keywords.
- 4 sitelinks, 4 callouts and 1 structured snippet.
- Bidding strategy and daily budget to start.
Give it to me as a table and export a CSV compatible with
Google Ads Editor to download.
05
Load the campaign into Google Ads (via API)
A real API upload, in PAUSED state. Atomic write, no spend.
prompt
Upload the CSV to Google Ads via the API in PAUSED state
(no spend): account [account id], manual CPC bidding,
[budget]/day. Validate policy and URLs before writing
and do it in a single atomic call. Do NOT enable it: I'll
switch it on after review.
06
Web report — the keyword study
The visual deliverable: volume, CPC and competition per group, in your branding.
prompt
Generate the keyword study as a web page in my branding:
group it by ad group and add volume, CPC and competition
from Keyword Planner (read-only), plus a projection of
clicks, cost and leads. Open it in the browser.
The agent builds, the human switches on

The upload is real and atomic, but always PAUSED: no ad is published and no money is spent. The agent builds, uploads and documents; the person reviews and decides what to switch on.

From 6 prompts to 2 commands

The real shortcut is to wrap this script as reusable skills: so it goes from six prompts to a couple of commands — "build SEM for this site" and "upload it to Google Ads, paused" — that anyone on the team can invoke.

Honest fine print
  • Skills = the assistant + its skills mechanism. There's no factory "SEM" button: you wrap the playbook yourself.
  • The API upload is real, but it's born PAUSED. Turning on spend is a human decision; set up the conversion (form submission) before switching it on.
  • The Final URL must match the landing domain: if your site redirects to another domain, point to the final destination or Google rejects the ad (DESTINATION_MISMATCH).
  • Real volumes and CPC come from Keyword Planner with your account (Google estimates). The report's clicks, cost and leads are our own projection, not served performance.
  • It's a build accelerator, not investment advice. How much and when to spend is a human call.
